27 March 2026

Bought a brand new Godrej 3-Star 1.5-Ton AC. Paid full price. Brand new. Not second hand. Not refurbished. New. With the naive belief that "new" means "works." Spoiler: It does not.
Investment Made
Late March – Early April 2026
Something Is Wrong. Very Wrong.
The AC that was supposed to fight the 52°C Delhi heat decided it had other plans. It wasn't cooling. A brand new appliance, installed days ago, already refusing to do its one job. Calls begin. The odyssey starts.
AC Not Cooling
10 April 2026
Technician Visit #1 — The Gas Gaslighting
After enormous effort — multiple calls, follow-ups, probably some light begging — a technician finally graced us with his presence. His diagnosis: gas leakage and insufficient gas. A brand new AC. With a gas leak. His solution? "Kal ladka aake dal dega." The gas man has no gas. Classic.
Promise: "Will Fix Tomorrow"
11–12 April 2026
"Kal" Comes. "Ladka" Does Not.
Tomorrow became the day after tomorrow. Then the day after that. The gas refill boy who was "definitely coming" apparently got lost in a dimension where commitments don't exist. More calls. More waiting. More hot nights.
Promise Evaporated
13 April 2026
Technician Visit #2 — The Plot Thickens
After a heroic amount of calling and requesting, a second technician arrived. This one had a different, worse diagnosis: inner coil defect — manufacturing defect. Not user damage. Not installation error. A defect that left the factory and got sealed inside a box sold as a new product. His response: "Ho jayega, par samay lagega."
Manufacturing Defect Confirmed
13 April – Present
The Long Wait — Patience as a Lifestyle
We said okay. We asked for a full replacement — because why should a customer accept a defective product that was literally brand new? The response was a masterpiece of non-commitment: "It'll happen, but it takes time." Time. As if time heals coil defects.
Replacement "Promised"
7 May 2026 — Today
Service Center Owner: Blocked. Shop Owner: Helpless.
Today I tried calling the service center owner. Again. Except — he stopped answering. The number that used to ring now goes unanswered. In all likelihood: blocked. Visited the shop where the AC was purchased. They called him. His answer? "Maine mail dala hai, par abhi tak revert nahi aaya. Main kaise karun?" You're paying ₹3,999/month for this kind of ambiguity. At least in summer.
Still. Not. Fixed.

The Godrej Dictionary

What They Say vs.
What They Mean.

A translation guide for navigating their customer service communication.

"Kal Ladka Aayega"
Official: The technician will visit tomorrow.
Actual: The gas cylinder is not in this dimension. Please hold for 3–72 business days. "Kal" is a flexible word.
"Manufacturing Defect"
Official: There is a defect in the product.
Actual: This is confirmed. This is real. This changes nothing. The clock for "samay lagega" has just begun.
"Ho Jayega"
Official: It will be done.
Actual: This is a deeply optimistic prediction about a future event whose timeline is controlled by no one you can contact.
"Maine Mail Dala Hai"
Official: I have escalated this.
Actual: A mail was sent. A response may come. Your issue may be addressed. All three events remain independent probabilities.
"Warranty Coverage"
Official: We cover defects within warranty period.
Actual: We cover defects by documenting them. Replacement happens on our timeline, which is measured in geological epochs.
"Samay Lagega"
Official: It will take some time.
Actual: "Some time" is doing extraordinary heavy lifting here. We regret to inform you it has been outsourced to infinity.
"Premium Product"
Official: High-quality air conditioner.
Actual: Premium packaging. Premium price. Premium coil defect. Premium silence from service. Premium everything — except cooling.
"Abhi Revert Nahi Aaya"
Official: We're awaiting a response.
Actual: The mail entered a void. The void does not reply. We are all the void now. Especially your room at 3 PM in May.
"Gas Leakage"
Official: Technical issue requiring refill.
Actual: A brand new AC has already failed. This is fine. The gas man has no gas. This is also fine. Come back tomorrow. Tomorrow is also fine.
